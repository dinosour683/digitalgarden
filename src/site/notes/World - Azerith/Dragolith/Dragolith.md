	A society that worships the many which are native to the area. The people of Dragolith have learned how to domesticate some dragons and can use them for transport, farming, combat, and even as pets. 

## Information

**Political System:** A representative democracy where every 500 days, a new leader is elected president. The presidents of Dragolith are all-powerful in the government. The current leader is a drakona woman named "Ranshin Sabertooth".

**Border:** [[World - Azerith/Azerith#The Gaping Maw\|The Gaping Maw]] is what draws the political border for Dragolith, as well as the habitat for the Dragons. Dragolith is noted as starting at the sea and ending at the far side of the Gaping Maw. 

**Capital:** The Capital City of Dragolith is known as '[[World - Azerith/Dragolith/The Spire\|The Spire]].' It is a medium-sized city with a huge spire in the centre. Throughout the spire (from the very top to underground), there are homes, stores, and other city amenities. 

**Climate:** A warm climate with small amounts of rain in the winter, with savannah-like plant and animal life.

**Defining Feature:** Dragons

## Geography 

The main geographical feature of Dragolith is [[World - Azerith/Azerith#The Gaping Maw\|The Gaping Maw]]. This is both the political border as well as the geographical border for Dragons. The Gaping Maw is hard to traverse with only one main path. This makes trade into the city easy to regulate and is one of the reasons for the high security in Dragolith. 

The one other point of interest in Dragolith is [[World - Azerith/Azerith#Sorrows Cave\|Sorrows Cave]]. No one dares to enter this cave or even settle around it. 

## Dragons

Dragons are a defining characteristic of Dragolith (as the name suggests). When people first discovered the corner of [[World - Azerith/Azerith\|Azerith]] past the gaping maw, they found creatures never seen before. Dragons.  They were wild and dangerous, roaming the land and killing anything deemed to be dangerous. As people settled in Dragolith, they slowly began domesticating dragons, using them for farming, fighting, and other utilities. Nowadays, dragons are not only domesticated but also worshipped. Many dragons are left to be wild and free as they are seen to be magical, godly creatures.
