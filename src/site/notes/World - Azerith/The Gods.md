	The world of Azerith is controlled by a pantheon of powerful gods. These gods are the reason for the creation of Azerith, as well as all of the natural and magical forces that affect Azerith. 

## Power Levels

In total, the gods are split into three levels of power: pantheon gods, minor gods, and spirit gods. Pantheon gods are the strongest gods, and spirit gods are the weakest.

### The Pantheon 

All of the strongest gods in Azerith are often considered to be a part of a pantheon (a group of gods that work together). The gods from the Pantheon were the original beings, and no one knows what came before them. The Pantheon created Azerith and all of the living things that populate it. There are 14 gods in The Pantheon, each with jurisdiction over a certain aspect of Azerith.

Although these gods are almost exclusively referred to as "gods" and not "goddesses" gods appear in many forms, some choosing to represent more masculinely and others more femininely.

### Minor Gods & Spirits

Minor gods are  weaker than the gods of The Pantheon and have control over something much more specific. For example the major god "luna" has control over the [[World - Azerith/Azerith#The Moons\|the moons]] while the weaker gods "skylight", "sealight", and "bloodlight" have control over one moon each. You can almost think of minor gods as demigods sorts. Some were created along side Azerith, some have always existed, and others used to be mortals. 

Spirits are a type of minor god that are even weaker than usual minor gods. The same rule applies where spirits have control over something very, very specific. 

It's important to acknowledge that even within the category of minor gods and spirits, there is still a range of power. The spirit of the Reani River would be much stronger than the spirit of a small creek (even though they are both spirits). 

There are countless minor gods and spirits in addition to the 14 main gods of the pantheon. 

Example:

- Whisper (God of the Sea) - Has dominion over every body of water. 
	- Respon (Minor God of Rivers) - Has dominion over every river
		- Reani (Spirit of the Reani River) - Has dominion over only the Reani River


### Devils and Demons

Devils and demons are evil creatures that are the main combatants of the generally benevolent gods. 

Demons are immortal creatures whose main goal is to oppose the gods. The best way to think about demons is to consider them as evil minor deities. Each demon has control over a certain aspect of the world (ex., hate, greed, poison), and there are countless amounts of demons. 

Although demons are considered to be immortal, you can kill them; however, the exact methods are unknown to most. 

Devils are henchmen of demons. They are technically mortal creatures, although they are tricky to kill. Weaker than most spirits, they still pose as formidable foes, as the demon that controls them will use magic to keep them alive. If you kill a devil, it will slowly regrow at the home of its demon. 

## Worshiping Gods

Gods in Azerith are worshipped in various ways. Almost everyone in Azerith worships the gods in some way, shape, or form. 

Across Azerith, there are countless temples dedicated to each god, run by priests and priestesses who have spent their entire lives dedicated to one specific god. These priests will spend their days worshipping that god and guiding people.  In addition to these priests, ordinary people visit these temples often for guidance, prayer, and to give offerings. 

During the month named after a god, it is believed that this god's power is amplified. This means that during this month (especially on the first), priests will celebrate their god even more than usual, often doing charity work and helping their local communities. 

Aside from worship in temples, many people pray during their everyday lives. If you need something that a god can help you with, you can make a prayer and give a small offering. Major prayers may necessitate a temple, but many small prayers can be done from anywhere.
